# 第002章：一对一对出现

## 原文

### 第1页

天下皆知美之为美，斯恶已；皆知善之为善，斯不善已。

故有无相生，难易相成，长短相形，高下相倾，音声相和，前后相随。

### 第2页

是以圣人处无为之事，行不言之教。

万物作焉而不辞，生而不有，为而不恃，功成而弗居。

夫唯弗居，是以不去。

## 释义

### 第3页

第二章先说一个很容易发生的事：当大家都说“这个才叫美”的时候，别的东西就容易被说成“不美”；当大家都抢着说“这个才叫善”的时候，别人也容易被推到“不善”的一边。

《道德经》不是说美不好、善不好，而是提醒我们：一旦只盯着比较和评判，心里就会多出很多争抢。

### 第4页

很多东西是一对一对显出来的。

有和无，难和易，长和短，高和低，声音和回声，前面和后面，都不是孤零零站着的。你拿出一根长竹竿，旁边那根才显得短；你站到台阶上，地面才显得低。

它们互相照见，互相帮对方出现。

### 第5页

所以，懂得“道”的人做事，不是用力抢在最前面，也不是一边做一边喊：“快看，这是我做的！”

他让事情自己长出来。帮助了，不把成果抓成自己的东西；做成了，不一定占住最高的位置。

这样，事情反而能留下来。因为大家记住的不是谁最会夸自己，而是那件事真的让人受用。

## 正文

### 第6页

第二天，空空村要搭一座花门。

花门要立在村口，迎接春天的集市。阿禾抱着昨天那本“名字本”，跑得比风还快。

“我来设计！”他说，“我要搭一座全村最美、最高、最长、最厉害的花门。”

他说完，在本子上写了四个大字：最美花门。

### 第7页

阿禾先挑竹竿。

他拿起一根长竹竿，说：“这根好！”

又拿起一根短竹竿，说：“这根不好。”

短竹竿靠在墙边，轻轻碰了一下水桶，发出“咚”的一声，好像不太服气。

阿禾又拿起一朵红花，说：“这朵美！”

旁边的小白花被风吹得点了点头，像是在问：“那我呢？”

### 第8页

不一会儿，村口热闹起来。

小石头说：“花门要高，高才气派。”

小兔弟弟说：“花门要宽，不然我的篮子过不去。”

小鸟说：“花门要轻，不然风一吹，它会先打喷嚏，再摔跤。”

阿禾把大家的话都写下来。

高。宽。轻。美。厉害。

本子看起来很忙，花门却一点也没有长出来。

### 第9页

阿禾决定先搭最高的。

他把两根长竹竿竖起来。竹竿很高，高到小鸟飞过去时，忍不住在上面停了一下。

可是花门下面太窄了。小兔弟弟拎着菜篮子一过，篮子被卡住，萝卜从里面滚出来，一路滚到老人的脚边。

老人正蹲在那里，把几根短竹竿排在地上。

他没有说话，只把一根长竹竿放在短竹竿旁边。

### 第10页

阿禾看见了。

长竹竿旁边，短竹竿更短了。

老人又把一块石头放在台阶上，再把另一块放在地上。

高的旁边，低的更低了。

老人敲了敲竹竿：“笃。”

山坡那边传来一声轻轻的回响：“笃。”

声音出来了，回声也跟着来了。

### 第11页

“它们好像一对一对出现。”阿禾小声说。

老人笑了笑，还是没有急着讲道理。

他把短竹竿横在两根长竹竿之间，又把细藤绕上去。短竹竿一横，花门忽然稳了。

小兔弟弟试着从下面走过去，菜篮子没有卡住。

小鸟从上面飞过去，花门也没有打喷嚏。

阿禾眨眨眼：“原来短的也有用。”

### 第12页

大家开始一起做。

小石头扶住左边的竹竿。小兔弟弟把花篮递上来。小鸟叼来一根细藤，骄傲得差点把自己也系上去。

阿禾本来想站在最前面指挥。

可是他发现，只要他一直喊“这边！那边！快一点！”大家的手就乱起来。

后来，他不喊了。

他看哪里松，就轻轻递一根藤；看哪里空，就放上一朵花；看谁够不着，就把凳子推过去。

### 第13页

太阳慢慢往西走。

花门终于搭好了。

它不是最高的。最高的竹竿被剪短了一点。

它也不是最长的。最长的藤绕成了一个小小的弯。

红花在一边，小白花在另一边。风吹过来，两边一起摇，像在互相点头。

村民们从花门下走过，都说：“真好看！”

阿禾听见了，心里亮了一下，像有人在本子里点起一盏小灯。

## 回扣

### 第14页

小石头问：“阿禾，要不要在花门上挂一块牌子，写‘阿禾做的’？”

阿禾摸了摸口袋，那里正好有一张纸条。

他想起昨天的小溪，也想起今天的短竹竿。

他把纸条拿出来，写了几个字，却没有挂到花门最高的地方。他把纸条贴在背面一根小竹竿上。

纸条上写着：大家一起做的。

这就是“功成而弗居”。做成了，也可以不站在最中间。

### 第15页

晚上，集市的灯亮起来。

花门站在村口，红花和白花都在灯光里微微发亮。人们从前面走进去，又从后面走出来；笑声响起，回声跟着跑。

阿禾在本子上写下：长和短互相显出来，高和低互相显出来，前和后互相跟着走。

第二章说的，不是不要美，也不是不做事。它是在说：万物常常互相照见，事情也常常靠大家一起成。真正留下来的，不是“谁最厉害”的牌子，而是那座大家都能走过的花门。

# Chapter 002: Things Appear in Pairs

## Original Text

### Page 1

When everyone knows beauty as beauty, ugliness has already appeared.

When everyone knows goodness as goodness, what is not good has already appeared.

So being and nonbeing give birth to each other.

Difficult and easy complete each other.

Long and short show each other.

High and low lean toward each other.

Tone and voice harmonize with each other.

Before and after follow each other.

### Page 2

Therefore the sage handles things through non-forcing,

and teaches without many words.

All things arise, and the sage does not refuse them.

He gives life, but does not possess.

He acts, but does not depend on his action.

The work is completed, but he does not stay on top of it.

Because he does not stay on top of it, it does not leave.

## Interpretation

### Page 3

Chapter Two begins with something that happens very easily.

When everyone says, "Only this is beautiful," other things can quickly be called "not beautiful." When everyone rushes to say, "Only this is good," other people may be pushed to the other side.

The Dao De Jing is not saying beauty is bad or goodness is bad. It is reminding us that when we care only about judging and comparing, our hearts can become busy with competition.

### Page 4

Many things appear in pairs.

Being and nonbeing, difficult and easy, long and short, high and low, sound and echo, before and after. None of them stands completely alone.

When you place a long bamboo pole beside a short one, the short one looks shorter. When you stand on a step, the ground looks lower.

They help each other appear.

### Page 5

So a person who understands the Dao does not always force himself to the front.

He does not work while shouting, "Look, I did this!"

He lets things grow in their own way. He helps, but does not grab the result as his own. When the work is finished, he does not have to take the highest place.

Then the work can remain, because people remember what was truly useful, not who praised himself the loudest.

## Main Text

### Page 6

The next day, Kongkong Village was going to build a flower gate.

The gate would stand at the village entrance to welcome the spring market. Ahe hugged the notebook from yesterday and ran faster than the wind.

"I will design it!" he said. "I will build the most beautiful, tallest, longest, most amazing flower gate in the whole village."

Then he wrote four big words in his notebook:

Most Beautiful Gate.

### Page 7

First, Ahe chose bamboo poles.

He picked up a long pole and said, "This one is good!"

Then he picked up a short pole and said, "This one is not good."

The short pole leaned against the wall and knocked a bucket with a small dong, as if it disagreed.

Ahe picked up a red flower and said, "This one is beautiful!"

Beside it, a small white flower nodded in the wind, as if asking, "What about me?"

### Page 8

Soon, the village entrance became noisy.

Little Stone said, "The gate should be tall. Tall looks grand."

Little Rabbit said, "The gate should be wide, or my basket will not pass through."

The little bird said, "The gate should be light. Otherwise, when the wind blows, it will sneeze first and fall down second."

Ahe wrote down everyone's words.

Tall. Wide. Light. Beautiful. Amazing.

The notebook looked busy, but the flower gate had not grown at all.

### Page 9

Ahe decided to build the tallest gate first.

He stood up two long bamboo poles. They were so tall that the little bird could not help landing on one.

But the space underneath was too narrow. When Little Rabbit walked through with his vegetable basket, the basket got stuck. Carrots rolled out and rolled all the way to the old man's feet.

The old man was crouching nearby, laying several short bamboo poles on the ground.

He did not speak. He only placed a long pole beside a short pole.

### Page 10

Ahe saw it.

Beside the long pole, the short pole looked even shorter.

Then the old man placed one stone on a step and another stone on the ground.

Beside the high one, the low one looked even lower.

The old man tapped a bamboo pole.

Dong.

From the hillside came a soft echo:

Dong.

When the sound appeared, the echo came with it.

### Page 11

"They seem to appear in pairs," Ahe whispered.

The old man smiled, but still did not hurry to explain.

He placed a short bamboo pole across the two long poles and wound thin vine around it. Once the short pole crossed the middle, the gate suddenly became steady.

Little Rabbit tried walking through. His basket did not get stuck.

The little bird flew over it. The gate did not sneeze.

Ahe blinked. "So the short one is useful too."

### Page 12

Everyone began to work together.

Little Stone held the left bamboo pole. Little Rabbit handed up the flower basket. The little bird brought a thin vine in its beak and looked so proud that it almost tied itself to the gate.

Ahe had wanted to stand in front and direct everyone.

But he noticed that whenever he kept shouting, "This way! That way! Faster!" everyone's hands became confused.

Later, he stopped shouting.

Where he saw something loose, he quietly handed over a vine. Where he saw an empty place, he added a flower. When someone could not reach, he pushed over a stool.

### Page 13

The sun slowly moved west.

At last, the flower gate was finished.

It was not the tallest. The tallest pole had been cut a little shorter.

It was not the longest. The longest vine had been wound into a small curve.

Red flowers stood on one side, and white flowers stood on the other. When the wind came, both sides swayed together, as if nodding to each other.

The villagers walked under the gate and said, "How beautiful!"

Ahe heard them, and something lit up inside him like a small lamp in his notebook.

## Return to the Original

### Page 14

Little Stone asked, "Ahe, should we hang a sign that says, 'Made by Ahe'?"

Ahe touched his pocket. There happened to be a small label inside.

He remembered yesterday's stream, and then today's short bamboo pole.

He took out the label and wrote a few words, but he did not hang it at the highest place. He stuck it on a small bamboo pole at the back.

It said:

Made Together.

This is "the work is completed, but he does not stay on top of it." When something is finished, Ahe does not have to stand in the middle.

### Page 15

At night, the market lights came on.

The flower gate stood at the village entrance. Red flowers and white flowers both glowed softly in the light. People walked in from the front and out from the back. Laughter rose, and echoes ran after it.

Ahe wrote in his notebook:

Long and short show each other. High and low show each other. Before and after follow each other.

Chapter Two is not saying we should reject beauty, and it is not saying we should do nothing. It says that things often shine by showing each other, and work is often completed by many hands. What remains is not the sign that says who was greatest, but the gate everyone can walk through.
