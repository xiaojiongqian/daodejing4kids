# 第002章：一对一对出现

## 原文

### 第1页

天下皆知美之为美，斯恶已；皆知善之为善，斯不善已。

故有无相生，难易相成，长短相形，高下相倾，音声相和，前后相随。

### 第2页

是以圣人处无为之事，行不言之教。

万物作焉而不辞，生而不有，为而不恃，功成而弗居。夫唯弗居，是以不去。

## 释义

### 第3页

当所有人都指着一件东西说"这才叫美"，别的样子就被推到了"不美"那一边。当所有人都抢着说"只有这样做才对"，别的做法就被推到了"不对"那一边。老子不是反对美和善——他只是提醒：忙着比较和评判，心里就会长出许多争抢。

### 第4页

万物常常一对一对显出来。有和无、难和易、长和短、高和低——都不是单独站着的。拿出长的，旁边那根才显得短；站到高处，低处才显得低。它们互相照见，互相帮对方出现。

### 第5页

声音和回声也是一对——敲一下，山谷回应，一个人成不了合奏。所以懂道的人不硬抢、不一直喊，让事情自己长。做成了，不把名字贴上去，事情反而留住了。

## 难词注解

### 第6页

「美」就是好看、让人喜欢的样子；「恶」在这里不是坏，是指不好看、不美。「善」就是好、做得对；「不善」是被推到"不好"那一边——不等于真的不好，只是被比较出来的。「有无相生」——有和无互相生出对方。像一只碗：碗壁是"有"，中间空的是"无"，没有那个空就装不了东西。

### 第7页

「难易相成」——难和易互相对照才成立。「长短相形」——长和短放在一起才显出彼此。「高下相倾」——高和低互相对照，谁也离不开谁。「音声相和」——单独一声叫"声"，声互相应和才变成音乐。「处无为之事」——做事不硬来，顺着事情自己的节奏帮它长。「功成而弗居」——做成了却不占着功劳不放；因为不霸占，那份好反而一直在。

## 正文

### 第8页

第二天，空空村要搭一座花门迎接春天。阿禾抱着本子跑过来，比风还快："我要搭一座全村最美的花门！"他在本子上写了四个大字：最美花门。

### 第9页

阿禾照着村长家年画上的花门画了一张图。他先挑竹竿——拿起最长最直的："这根好！"短的被推到一边，撞翻水桶"咚"一声。又挑花——大红花放最前面，旁边一朵小白花被风吹得点了点头，他却没有看见。

### 第10页

阿禾照着画上的样子搭，花门歪了——左边高，右边矮。"为什么照着最美的做，反而不好看？"老人走过来讲了个故事：西施心口疼时捧心皱眉，大家看了更觉动人；东施跟着学，把邻居全吓跑了。小兔噗嗤笑了："她以为美在那个动作上，不知道那个动作长在西施身上才动人。"

### 第11页

老人把一根长竹竿放在短竹竿旁边——长的旁边，短的更短了，但也显得更结实、更稳当。又把一块石头放台阶上、一块放地上——高的旁边，低的更低了，但一伸手就够得着。他拿起竹竿在石头上轻轻一敲——"笃"，山坡那边传来回应。它们都在互相帮忙。

### 第12页

老人把短竹竿横在两根长竹竿之间，用藤绕了几圈。花门忽然稳了——不歪了，两边对称了。小兔从下面走过去，菜篮子没有卡住。阿禾眨了眨眼："原来短的也有用。"

### 第13页

大家一起动手。阿禾本想站在前面指挥，可他一喊"这边、那边"，大家反而手忙脚乱。他想起老人没有讲大道理，只敲了一下竹子。他不再喊了——看哪里松就递根藤，看哪里空就放朵花，不说话，大家反而懂了。

### 第14页

太阳往西走，花门搭好了。它不是最高的，也不是年画上的样子——但红花在一边，白花在另一边，风吹过来两边一起摇。村民们从花门下走过，都说："真好看！"阿禾心里亮了一下——不是"最美"，但是很真。

## 回扣

### 第15页

小石头问："要不要挂一块牌子，写'阿禾做的'？"阿禾摸了摸口袋里的纸条，走到花门背面，贴在一根不起眼的小竹竿上。纸条上写着：大家一起做的。

### 第16页

晚上，集市的灯亮了。花门立在村口，红花和白花都在暖黄的灯下微微发亮。阿禾合上本子，抬头看花门——月光下，长短竹竿的影子落在地上，一长一短，像一对好朋友。

# Chapter 002: Things Appear in Pairs

## Original Text

### Page 1

All in the world know the beauty of the beautiful, and in doing this they have (the idea of) what ugliness is; they all know the skill of the skilful, and in doing this they have (the idea of) what the want of skill is.

So it is that existence and non-existence give birth the one to (the idea of) the other; that difficulty and ease produce the one (the idea of) the other; that length and shortness fashion out the one the figure of the other; that (the ideas of) height and lowness arise from the contrast of the one with the other; that the musical notes and tones become harmonious through the relation of one with another; and that being before and behind give the idea of one following another.

### Page 2

Therefore the sage manages affairs without doing anything, and conveys his instructions without the use of speech.

All things spring up, and there is not one which declines to show itself; they grow, and there is no claim made for their ownership; they go through their processes, and there is no expectation (of a reward for the results). The work is accomplished, and there is no resting in it (as an achievement).

The work is done, but how no one can see;
'Tis this that makes the power not cease to be.

> — Translated by James Legge (1891), *The Texts of Taoism*, Sacred Books of the East, Vol. 39. Public domain.

## Interpretation

### Page 3

When everyone points at the same thing and says "Only this is beautiful," everything else gets pushed to the "not beautiful" side. When everyone says "Only this is right," others get pushed to the "not right" side. Laozi is not against beauty or goodness — he is reminding us: the moment we get busy comparing and judging, our hearts fill up with competition.

### Page 4

So many things appear in pairs: being and nonbeing, hard and easy, long and short, high and low. None stands completely alone. Hold up a long pole, and the one beside it looks shorter; stand on a high step, and the ground below looks lower. They help each other appear.

### Page 5

Sound and echo are a pair too — tap once, the hillside answers; one person alone cannot make harmony. So the sage does not push or shout. He lets things grow. When the work is done, he does not paste his name on it — and because he does not cling, the work stays.

## Vocabulary Notes

### Page 6

**Beauty of the beautiful.** Legge's phrase for the idea that only one thing counts as beautiful. The moment you set that standard, you create its opposite: ugliness.

**The skilful / want of skill.** When you declare what is "skilled," you instantly create "not skilled enough." Labeling one side creates the other.

**Existence and non-existence give birth the one to (the idea of) the other.** Think of a bowl: the clay walls are "being," the hollow is "nonbeing." Without the hollow, it holds nothing.

### Page 7

**Difficulty and ease produce the one (the idea of) the other.** Tying shoelaces felt impossible the first time — hard taught you what easy means.

**Length and shortness fashion out the one the figure of the other.** Long and short reveal each other only side by side.

**(The ideas of) height and lowness arise from the contrast of the one with the other.** Stand on a peak, the valley looks low; stand in the valley, the peak looks high.

**Manages affairs without doing anything.** Not forcing, not meddling — working with the natural flow.

**No resting in it (as an achievement).** Step aside instead of sitting on your work. Because you do not cling, it lives on.

## Main Text

### Page 8

The next day, Kongkong Village was going to build a flower gate to welcome spring. Ahe came running, faster than the wind: "I will build the most beautiful flower gate in the whole village!" He wrote four big words in his notebook: Most Beautiful Gate.

### Page 9

Ahe copied a flower gate from the New Year painting at the village head's house. He picked up the longest, straightest bamboo pole: "This one is good!" The short one was shoved aside, knocking over a bucket with a *dong*. He held up a big red blossom for the front — beside him, a small white flower nodded in the wind, but he did not see it.

### Page 10

Ahe built it exactly like the painting — but the gate leaned, left high and right low. "Why does copying the most beautiful one turn out so wrong?" The old man told a story: Xi Shi furrowed her brow in pain and looked even more lovely; Dong Shi copied the gesture, and the neighbors all fled. Little Rabbit burst out laughing: "She thought beauty was in the gesture, not in who Xi Shi was."

### Page 11

The old man placed a long bamboo pole beside a short one — the short looked shorter, but sturdier. He set one stone on a step, one on the ground — the low one looked lower, but you could reach it easily. He tapped a pole against a stone: *Dok.* The hillside answered softly. They were all helping each other.

### Page 12

The old man laid the short pole across two long ones and wound vine around it. The gate steadied — no longer tilted, both sides balanced. Little Rabbit walked through without his basket getting stuck. Ahe blinked: "So the short one is useful too."

### Page 13

Everyone began working together. Ahe wanted to stand at the front and direct — but the more he shouted "This way! That way!", the more tangled everyone became. He remembered the old man had not lectured, only tapped a bamboo pole. He stopped shouting — when he saw a loose spot he handed a vine, when he saw a gap he placed a flower. Without words, everyone understood.

### Page 14

The sun moved west. The flower gate was finished — not the tallest, not like the painting, but red on one side, white on the other, both swaying together in the wind. The villagers walked beneath it: "How beautiful!" Ahe felt something light up inside — not "most beautiful," but real.

## Return to the Original

### Page 15

Little Stone asked, "Should we hang a sign that says 'Made by Ahe'?" Ahe touched the label in his pocket, then walked to the back of the gate and stuck it on a quiet little pole. It read: Made by Everyone Together.

### Page 16

At night, the market lights came on. The flower gate stood at the village entrance, red and white blossoms glowing softly in the warm yellow light. Ahe closed his notebook and looked up — in the moonlight, the shadows of the long pole and the short pole stretched out on the ground, one long, one short, like a pair of good friends.
