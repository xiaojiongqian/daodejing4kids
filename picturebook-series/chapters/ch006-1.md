# 第006-1章：不说话的山谷

## 原文

### 第1页

谷神不死，是谓玄牝。
玄牝之门，是谓天地根。
绵绵若存，用之不勤。

## 释义

### 第2页

“谷神”不是说山谷里住着会说话的神。王弼说，山谷中间空空、无形无影，却能让风、水、声音从里面经过。

它低着、空着、不争着占有任何东西，所以万物能在其中来去；这就是“不死”的妙用。

### 第3页

“玄牝”可以理解为幽深的生命之母。它不是一个人，也不是一个地点，而是万物生发的深处。

老子用“门”和“根”来形容它：万物从那里显现，也靠那看不见的源头继续生长。

### 第4页

“绵绵若存”是说，它细细地、不断地在那里，好像有，又看不见。

像山泉从石缝里流出，不轰隆，却能一年一年养活草木。

### 第5页

“用之不勤”不是不用，也不是偷懒。河上公说，用气要宽舒，不要急促劳累。

放到生活里，就是不把生命源头当成一只硬拧的水龙头；该用时用，该停时停，泉才不浑。

## 难词注解

### 第6页

**谷神**：山谷空处的灵妙作用。空，不是死；空能回声、藏风、汇水。

**玄**：幽深、看不清，却不是没有。

**牝**：古人用来表示能生养的一面。这里可理解为生命的母性源头。

### 第7页

**门**：出入的地方。这里指万物生出的幽深开口。

**天地根**：天地万物的根源。不是一根看得见的树根，而是更深的来处。

**不勤**：不用得太急太累。保持宽舒，才不容易耗尽。

## 正文

### 第8页

雨后，阿禾跟老人走进山谷。两边石壁高高站着，中间却空出一条凉凉的路。

阿禾轻轻喊了一声，声音从远处又回来了。

### 第9页

小石头睁大眼：“山谷里有人回答吗？”

老人说：“不是有人藏着。是山谷空，声音才有地方走，也有地方回来。”

### 第10页

他们继续往里走，听见一线细水。水从石缝里出来，细得像银丝。

它没有瀑布那样大声，却把苔藓养绿，把树根润湿。

### 第11页

小石头想拿铲子把泉眼挖大。“这样水会更多吧？”

老人拦住他：“太用力，泥会浑，泉也可能被伤到。细细流，反而流得久。”

### 第12页

阿禾蹲下看泉。水像在，又像只是亮了一下；用手一捧，又真的凉在掌心。

他忽然懂了“若存”：好像抓不住，却一直在。

### 第13页

傍晚，山谷里起了雾。雾不急着跑，也不急着证明自己。

路边的草尖挂着水珠。山谷沉默，却没有死，它正在悄悄生养。

## 回扣

### 第14页

第六章说的“谷神”，就是这种空而能应、静而能生的妙用。

它不站出来说“我是根”。可正因它不占满自己，风从这里过，水从这里出，草木从这里得滋养。

### 第15页

阿禾写下：真正深的东西，不一定吵；真正久的东西，不一定硬用力。

像山谷，低低地空着，所以能应；像泉水，绵绵地在，所以能生。

# Chapter 006-1: The Valley That Does Not Speak

## Original Text

### Page 1

The valley spirit dies not, aye the same;
The female mystery thus do we name.
Its gate, from which at first they issued forth,
Is called the root from which grew heaven and earth.
Long and unbroken does its power remain,
Used gently, and without the touch of pain.

## Interpretation

### Page 2

"Valley spirit" does not mean a talking ghost lives in the valley. Wang Bi says the center of a valley is empty and shapeless, yet wind, water, and sound can pass through it.

It stays low, open, and does not possess anything. Therefore things can come and go through it; this is the living use that "does not die."

### Page 3

"Female mystery" can be understood as the deep, life-giving mother-source. It is not a person or a place, but the hidden depth from which things arise.

Laozi uses "gate" and "root" for it: things appear through it and keep growing from an unseen source.

### Page 4

"Long and unbroken" means it is fine, continuous, and quietly there. It seems present, though we cannot hold it.

Like a spring coming through stone, it does not roar, yet year after year it nourishes green life.

### Page 5

"Used gently" does not mean never used, and it is not laziness. Heshang Gong says breath should be wide and easy, not rushed and exhausted.

In daily life, this means not treating the life-source like a faucet twisted hard. Use when it is time, rest when it is time, and the spring will not muddy.

## Vocabulary Notes

### Page 6

**valley spirit** — The living usefulness of an empty valley: echo, wind, water, and response.

**female mystery** — A deep, hidden, life-giving source.

**gate** — A place where things pass in and out.

### Page 7

**root** — The deep source from which life grows.

**long and unbroken** — Continuing softly without being cut off.

**used gently** — Used without strain, hurry, or harshness.

## Main Text

### Page 8

After rain, Ahe walked with the old man into a valley. Tall stone walls stood on both sides, while a cool empty path opened in the middle.

Ahe called softly, and the voice came back from far away.

### Page 9

Little Stone's eyes widened. "Is someone answering inside the valley?"

"No one is hiding there," said the old man. "The valley is hollow, so sound has room to travel and return."

### Page 10

They walked farther in and heard a thread of water. It came through a crack in the stone, thin as silver thread.

It was not loud like a waterfall, yet it greened the moss and moistened the roots.

### Page 11

Little Stone wanted to dig the spring wider with a shovel. "Wouldn't that make more water?"

The old man stopped him. "Too much force muddies the water and may hurt the spring. A fine flow can last longer."

### Page 12

Ahe crouched to watch the spring. The water seemed almost not there, just a flash of light; yet when he cupped it, it was cool in his palms.

He understood "as if present": not easy to grasp, yet always here.

### Page 13

At dusk, mist rose in the valley. It did not rush away or try to prove itself.

Drops hung on the grass tips. The valley was silent, but not dead; it was quietly nourishing life.

## Return to the Original

### Page 14

The "valley spirit" in Chapter Six is this: empty yet responsive, still yet life-giving.

It does not announce, "I am the root." Because it does not fill itself, wind passes here, water comes here, and plants are nourished here.

### Page 15

Ahe wrote: What is truly deep does not have to be noisy. What lasts long does not have to push hard.

Like a valley, it stays low and open, so it can respond. Like spring water, it continues softly, so it can give life.
