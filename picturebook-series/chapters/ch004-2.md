# 第004-2章：不刺眼的光和看不见的深处

## 典故引入

### 第1页

庄子讲过“空船”的故事。一个人坐船过河，被一只无人船轻轻撞到，多半只是撑开它。

可如果船上站着一个人，他就容易生气，觉得对方故意撞他。

### 第2页

撞击没有变，变的是心里多出一个“他在冒犯我”的尖角。

第四章说“挫其锐”。不是把人磨成没骨头，而是先看见锋芒从哪里长出来。

### 第3页

唐朝安史之乱后，郭子仪功劳大到能震动朝廷。皇帝听了谗言，怕这位老将军握兵太重，忽然收走他的兵权。

这是最容易刺起来的时刻：有功被疑，手下有兵，胸中有委屈。

### 第4页

郭子仪没有召旧部，也没有拍桌自辩。他奉命去修皇陵，又把旧诏书和机密文书一件件交还。

这等于把可能被误会成兵刃的东西，全放到阳光下。

### 第5页

皇帝看见以后，知道他没有把功劳变成要挟。疑心的结松了一点。

“解纷”有时不是赢过对方，而是让对方再也抓不到可以害怕的影子。

### 第6页

后来，仇人挖了郭子仪父亲的坟。朝廷都以为他必定报复，事情可能牵出一串杀戮。

他却先说自己带兵多年，士兵也曾扰民伤墓；现在遇到此事，是自己的德行不够。

### 第7页

这一句话，把复仇的火往回收。别人本来等着看刀光，结果看见的是自省。

锋芒被挫下去，纷乱就没有继续滚大的坡。

### 第8页

李泌面对安史叛军，也懂这个道理。叛军来势凶猛，像一把刚磨亮的刀。

他看出那把刀的弱处：他们多为抢掠而来，锐而不能久。

### 第9页

他的计策不是正面硬撞，而是断要路、守关键，让敌军在范阳和长安之间来回奔命。

锋芒若没人迎着去撞，就会在奔跑、饥疲和拖延中自己钝下来。

### 第10页

可皇帝急着收复两京，没有完全听从。眼前捷报很快，深处的乱根却没有除净。

这就是第四章的警告：只砍表面的结，线头还会在暗处重新缠住。

### 第11页

再看荷兰画家维米尔。他生活在小城代尔夫特，常画的不是英雄和王冠，而是窗边的牛奶、信和安静的脸。

他把光放得很轻，轻到像怕惊动尘土。

### 第12页

《戴珍珠耳环的少女》没有战场，也没有大典。只是一个女孩回头，暗处有一颗小小的珍珠。

可几百年后，人们仍在画前停下，因为柔光没有逼人，却把人请进了深处。

### 第13页

这就是“和其光，同其尘”。光不是灭掉，而是不再炫耀自己。

它走进普通房间，普通人才被照亮；尘土也成了画的一部分。

### 第14页

贝多芬的故事更沉。一个作曲家，后来几乎听不见外面的声音。

对音乐家来说，这像整座世界慢慢关门：掌声远了，乐队远了，连自己的琴声也远了。

### 第15页

他没有把痛苦变成空喊。许多声音退到外面后，更深的声音在心里继续生长。

晚年《第九交响曲》首演时，他背对观众，听不见掌声，却看见所有人站了起来。

### 第16页

那一刻，“湛兮似或存”变得很清楚。真正深的声音，外面好像没有了，里面却还在。

它不靠耳朵证明自己，却抵达许多人的心。

## 回扣

### 第17页

这些故事不是散开的。空船先让“我被冒犯”的尖角落下；郭子仪让皇帝和仇人的疑火失去燃料。

这叫挫锐，不是折断力量，而是让力量不再伤人。

### 第18页

李泌的谋略是解纷：不和锋芒硬撞，而是看见线头在哪里、燃料在哪里。

真正深的解法，常不是快刀一挥，而是不让乱线继续结成死结。

### 第19页

维米尔和贝多芬讲和光同尘、湛兮似存。一个把光放进普通房间，一个在失聪后听见更深处。

深的东西不急着站上高台，却能长久地照人、动人。

### 第20页

第四章说，道不塞满自己，所以能用；不刺伤世界，所以能入世。

阿禾写下：有空，水才来；不尖，结才松；光柔，尘土也能亮。

# Chapter 004-2: Gentle Light and the Invisible Depth

## Allusion

### Page 1

Zhuangzi tells the story of an empty boat. A person crossing a river is bumped by a boat with no one inside, and he will probably just push it away.

But if someone stands in that boat, anger rises. He thinks, "You hit me on purpose."

### Page 2

The bump has not changed. What changed is that a sharp point has appeared in the heart: "He is offending me."

Chapter Four says, "Blunt sharpness." This does not mean becoming weak. It means first seeing where sharpness grows.

### Page 3

After the An Lushan Rebellion, Guo Ziyi's merit was great enough to shake the Tang court. The emperor heard slander and feared that this old general held too much military power.

This was the moment when anyone could grow sharp: merit was doubted, troops had followed him, and grievance filled the chest.

### Page 4

Guo Ziyi did not summon old soldiers or slam the table. He obeyed an order to repair the imperial tombs, then returned old imperial letters and secret documents one by one.

It was as if he placed every object that could be mistaken for a blade into the sunlight.

### Page 5

When the emperor saw this, he knew Guo had not turned merit into a threat. The knot of suspicion loosened a little.

Sometimes "unraveling complications" is not defeating the other person. It is leaving him no shadow to fear.

### Page 6

Later, an enemy dug up Guo Ziyi's father's grave. The court expected Guo to take revenge, and revenge could have pulled many people into killing.

But Guo first blamed himself: in years of war, soldiers under him had also harmed people's graves. Now this grief showed that his own virtue was not enough.

### Page 7

That sentence pulled the fire of revenge back inward. People expected to see blades, but they saw self-examination.

When sharpness was blunted, disorder had no slope on which to keep rolling.

### Page 8

Li Bi understood this too when he faced the rebel armies. The rebels came fiercely, like a newly sharpened blade.

He saw the weakness inside that blade: many came for plunder, and fierce plunder could not patiently endure.

### Page 9

His plan was not to crash into them head-on. He would cut key roads and make them run between Fanyang and Chang'an while loyal forces held steady.

If no one meets sharpness with sharpness, it can grow dull through running, hunger, and delay.

### Page 10

But the emperor wanted the two capitals back quickly and did not fully follow Li Bi. Quick victory came, but the deeper root of disorder remained.

This is Chapter Four's warning: if only the surface knot is cut, the hidden thread may tangle again.

### Page 11

Now look at the Dutch painter Vermeer. He lived in the small city of Delft and often painted not heroes or crowns, but milk by a window, letters, and quiet faces.

He placed light very gently, as if he did not want to startle the dust.

### Page 12

*Girl with a Pearl Earring* has no battlefield and no ceremony. It is only a girl turning her head, with a small pearl in the dark.

Yet centuries later, people still stop before it, because the soft light does not force them; it invites them inward.

### Page 13

This is "tempering brightness and joining the dust." The light is not put out; it stops showing itself off.

It enters an ordinary room, so ordinary people are illuminated; even dust becomes part of the painting.

### Page 14

Beethoven's story is heavier. A composer later became almost unable to hear the outer world.

For a musician, this was like the whole world slowly closing its doors: applause moved away, orchestras moved away, even his own piano moved away.

### Page 15

He did not turn pain into empty shouting. When many outer sounds retreated, a deeper sound kept growing within.

In his later years, at the first performance of the Ninth Symphony, he stood with his back to the audience. He could not hear the applause, but he saw everyone standing.

### Page 16

At that moment, "pure and still, as if present" became clear. The deepest sound seemed gone outside, yet it remained within.

It did not prove itself through ears, but it reached many hearts.

## Return to the Original

### Page 17

These stories are not separate. The empty boat lets the sharp point of "I have been offended" fall away; Guo Ziyi removes fuel from the emperor's suspicion and his own revenge.

This is blunting sharpness: not breaking strength, but keeping strength from wounding.

### Page 18

Li Bi's strategy is unraveling complications: do not crash into sharpness; see where the thread and the fuel are.

The deepest solution is often not one fast cut. It is keeping tangled threads from becoming a dead knot.

### Page 19

Vermeer and Beethoven teach tempering brightness, joining the dust, and being present in hidden depth. One places light in ordinary rooms; one hears deeper after losing hearing.

Deep things do not hurry to stand on high platforms, yet they can shine and move people for a long time.

### Page 20

Chapter Four says Dao does not stuff itself full, so it can be used; it does not wound the world, so it can enter the world.

Ahe wrote: Where there is room, water comes. Where sharpness softens, knots loosen. Where light is gentle, even dust can shine.
