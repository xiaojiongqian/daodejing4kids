# 第007-2章：最后面的人，最前面的心

## 典故引入

### 第1页

有一家户外用品公司叫 Patagonia。创始人伊冯·乔伊纳德一开始并不想做“商人”，只是给攀岩朋友做耐用的工具和衣服。

公司越做越大，他也看见一个问题：生意如果只想着多卖、多赚，也会耗伤山、河和人的生活。

### 第2页

Patagonia 选择把“保护地球”放到赚钱前面。从 1985 年起，它长期把销售额的 1% 捐给环保行动。

这看起来像先把钱让出去。可是顾客看见，它不是只来掏口袋，也在认真守护大家共同的家。

### 第3页

2022 年，乔伊纳德一家把公司所有权转入新的使命结构。除再投入公司之外，未来利润会流向保护自然的工作。

它没有先问“怎样让老板更富”，而是先问“怎样让山、河、海和人还能继续生活”。钱不再坐在最中间，它变成服务大事的水流。

### 第4页

这里的关键，不是公司从此不需要赚钱。它仍要卖衣服、付工资、改进产品，也要让生意站得住。

只是利润不再像王一样坐在王座上。它被放进一个更大的问题里：共同的家怎样继续被使用、被热爱？

### 第5页

所以顾客买到的不只是一件外套。他们也买到一种信任：这家公司不会把共同的家当成一次性矿山。

信任一旦长出来，比广告更耐久。

### 第6页

南非的曼德拉，被关在罗本岛监狱二十七年。白天，他在采石场工作，石灰岩的反光刺伤了眼睛。

如果只按个人痛苦来说，他有太多理由把仇恨放在最前面。

### 第7页

可是出狱以后，他没有让国家跟着自己的伤口一起燃烧。他邀请旧日狱卒参加就职典礼，也推动真相与和解。

他没有忘记伤害，却把南非的未来放在自己的痛苦前面。这样保存下来的，不只是他的生命，还有一个国家继续生活的可能。

### 第8页

他若把总统位置变成复仇的椅子，支持他的人也许会痛快一阵，国家却可能继续撕裂。

他把自己的伤痛往后放，让共同未来坐到前面。于是，旧日敌人也有了放下武器的台阶。

### 第9页

中国的范仲淹写《岳阳楼记》，也把自己的心放到很远很大。

他写洞庭湖的阴晴风雨，不只是在写一座楼，也是在写一个人怎样看天下。

### 第10页

他留下“先天下之忧而忧，后天下之乐而乐”。忧的时候站在前面，享乐的时候退到后面。

这句话不是漂亮口号。一个人若真把天下忧乐放在自己前面，他的心就不再困在一己得失里。

### 第11页

范仲淹不是没有自己的苦乐。他也会被贬，也会孤独。

可他的心不把个人升沉当成全部，所以一座楼、一片湖，都能打开到天下人的忧乐。

### 第12页

这三则故事有同一条线：越急着保住自己的利润、伤痛和得失，别人越会防备，力量也越耗在相争里。

若先守住共同的路，自然、国家和天下被放在前面，信任才有地方生根。

### 第13页

所以“后其身而身先”不是道德标语，而是人心的因果。

后退一步，不是消失，而是让众人看见：这里没有抢夺。人一放心，就愿意靠近，也愿意跟随。

## 回扣

### 第14页

“外其身而身存”也有因果。若只保住利润，Patagonia 可能失去自然根基和顾客信任；若只保住仇恨，曼德拉可能失去国家。

若只保住一己得失，范仲淹也只是一位官员，不会成为后人记住的宽心。

### 第15页

“不自生”不是不要自己的生命，而是不把孤立的小我当成生命中心。天地生养万物，却不把万物圈成自己的财产。

天地的生命，不停在一个“我”里，而在万物的生生不息里延续。这就是“故能长生”。

### 第16页

所以老子不是在教“退后换好处”。他是在说：小我若只为自己生，必与世界相争；小我若进入更大的生命，就借共同生命而长久。

这就是为什么天地“不自生”，故能长生；圣人“后其身”，反而身先。

### 第17页

老子又说，外其身而身存；无私，故能成其私。“外”不是抛弃自己，而是不把得失当作生命边界。

小我回到更大的生命里，成就的不是小算盘，而是使命、共同生活和宽广的心。

### 第18页

在孩子的生活里，也能练习：排队、分东西、承担责任时，先看整体怎样更好，再看自己怎样安住其中。

阿禾写下：小我不是被抹掉；小我回到大我，像一滴水回到河海，跟生生不息的生命一起长久。

# Chapter 007-2: The Last Person, the Foremost Heart

## Allusion

### Page 1

There is an outdoor company called Patagonia. Its founder, Yvon Chouinard, did not begin by wanting to be a "businessman"; he made durable tools and clothes for climbers.

As the company grew, he saw a problem: if business only tries to sell more and earn more, it can also wear down mountains, rivers, and people's lives.

### Page 2

Patagonia chose to put protecting the planet ahead of chasing money. Since 1985, it has long given one percent of sales to environmental work.

That looked like giving money away first. But customers saw that the company was not only reaching for their pockets; it was trying to guard a shared home.

### Page 3

In 2022, Chouinard's family transferred the company's ownership into a new mission structure. After reinvestment, future profits would flow toward protecting nature.

It did not ask first, "How can the owner become richer?" It asked, "How can mountains, rivers, seas, and people keep living?" Money no longer sat in the center; it became a stream serving a larger matter.

### Page 4

The point is not that a company no longer needs to earn money. Patagonia still has to sell clothes, pay workers, improve products, and keep the business standing.

But profit is no longer like a king on the throne. It is placed inside a larger question: how can our shared home remain usable and loved?

### Page 5

So customers are not only buying a jacket. They are also trusting a promise: this company will not treat the shared home as a disposable mine.

When trust grows, it lasts longer than advertising.

### Page 6

Nelson Mandela in South Africa spent twenty-seven years in prison on Robben Island. By day, he worked in a stone quarry, where white limestone glare damaged his eyes.

If he had measured everything only by personal pain, he had many reasons to place hatred in front.

### Page 7

But after leaving prison, he did not make the country burn with his wound. He invited former jailers to his inauguration and supported truth and reconciliation.

He did not forget harm, but he placed South Africa's future before his private hurt. What was preserved was not only his life, but the possibility of a country living together.

### Page 8

If Mandela had turned the presidency into a chair of revenge, his supporters might have felt relief for a while, but the country could have torn again.

He put his own wound behind and let the shared future sit in front. Then even former enemies had a step on which to put down their weapons.

### Page 9

In China, Fan Zhongyan wrote "The Record of Yueyang Tower" with a heart that looked far and wide.

He wrote of the wind, rain, brightness, and darkness over Dongting Lake. He was not only writing about a tower; he was writing about how a person sees the world.

### Page 10

He left the thought: be first to worry for the world, and last to enjoy its joy. Stand in front for sorrow; step behind for pleasure.

This is not a pretty slogan. If a person truly places the world's worry and joy before his own gain and loss, his heart is no longer trapped inside one small self.

### Page 11

Fan Zhongyan was not without sorrow or joy of his own. He was demoted too, and he knew loneliness.

But his heart did not make personal rise and fall the whole world. So one tower and one lake could open into the worries and joys of all under heaven.

### Page 12

These three stories share one line: the more someone rushes to protect profit, wounds, or personal gain and loss, the more others defend against him, and strength is spent in struggle.

When someone protects the shared road first, nature, country, and the world are placed in front. Then trust has a place to take root.

### Page 13

"Putting oneself behind and being found in front" is not only a moral slogan. It is a cause and effect in the human heart.

Stepping back is not disappearing. It lets others see: there is no grabbing here. When people feel safe, they are willing to come close and follow.

## Return to the Original

### Page 14

"Treating the self as outside and being preserved" has a cause and effect too. If Patagonia had only protected profit, it might have lost its natural ground and customers' trust; if Mandela had only protected hatred, he might have lost the country.

If Fan Zhongyan had only protected personal gain and loss, he would have been only an official, not a wide heart remembered by later generations.

### Page 15

"Not living for themselves" does not mean having no life of one's own. It means not making the separate little self the center of life. Heaven and earth give life to all things, but do not turn all things into their property.

Their life does not stop inside one "me"; it continues in the living of all things. This is why they endure.

### Page 16

So Laozi is not teaching "step back and get benefits." He is saying: when the little self lives only for itself, it fights the world; when it enters a larger life, it endures through shared life.

That is why heaven and earth "do not live for themselves," and so endure; that is why the sage puts himself behind, and is found in front.

### Page 17

Laozi also says the sage treats himself as outside, and so he is preserved; having no private ends, his ends are fulfilled. "Outside" does not mean throwing yourself away. It means not treating gain and loss as the border of life.

When the little self returns to a larger life, what is fulfilled is not a small scheme, but purpose, shared life, and a wide heart.

### Page 18

Children can practice this too. In line, sharing, or taking responsibility, first ask how the whole can become better, then find your place inside it.

Ahe wrote: The little self is not erased. It returns to the greater self, like a drop returning to the river and sea, enduring with the life that keeps flowing.
