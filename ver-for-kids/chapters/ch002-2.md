# 第002-2章：西施的眉头和山谷的回声

## 典故引入

### 第1页

庄子讲过一个故事：春秋时有位女子叫西施，大家都说她很美。有一天她心口疼，轻轻按着胸口，眉头也皱了起来。

路人看见，仍觉得她动人。那不是故意摆出的姿态，而是身体难受时自然流露出的样子。

### 第2页

同村的东施看见了，以为“按胸口、皱眉头”就是美。她也照着做，还特意走到人多的地方。

人们没有觉得亲近，反而吓得躲开。后来，这个故事就叫“东施效颦”。

### 第3页

这个故事不是为了嘲笑谁长得不好。它提醒我们：只学别人的外样，不知道那个样子从哪里来，就会越学越别扭。

这正好照见第二章开头：如果大家只认一种“美”，许多本来不同的样子，就被挤到“不美”那边去了。

## 正文

### 第4页

阿禾听完故事，兴冲冲办了一场“最美皱眉大会”。小兔皱眉，小鸟皱眉，小石头也把眉毛挤成一团。

没多久，大家的脸都酸了。院子里明明有阳光，却像飘来一小片闷闷的阴天。

### 第5页

这时，泥盆里的一株豆苗顶开土，露出一片嫩叶。小兔忘了皱眉，忽然咧嘴笑了。

阿禾看见那个笑，心里一下亮了：原来动人的样子，常常不是练出来的。

### 第6页

老人说：“西施皱眉动人，是因为那一刻是真的。东施错在把别人的自然，硬贴到自己脸上。”

阿禾看看大家酸酸的眉毛，赶紧把“最美皱眉大会”的牌子收了起来。

### 第7页

下午，他们走到山谷口。小石头拍了一下手，啪，声音飞进谷里。

过了一会儿，山谷把声音送回来：啪。孩子们都笑了，原来声音也会有来有往。

### 第8页

小鸟吹一声高音，小兔敲一声低鼓。高音没有盖住低鼓，低鼓也没有抢走高音。

一个在上面亮一下，一个在下面托一下。听着听着，像有一座小桥在空气里搭起来。

### 第9页

古书《礼记·乐记》说，心被外面的事物触动，就会发出声。声和声互相应答，慢慢有了节奏，就成了音。

阿禾听懂了一点：一个声音独自响着，还不是音乐。它要听见别的声音，才知道自己该落在哪里。

### 第10页

他们试着只敲一只鼓，咚，咚，咚。声音很响，可听久了有点单薄。

再加上竹笛、石片和拍手，声音开始互相让路。小曲子不是谁一个人做出来的，是从大家中间长出来的。

### 第11页

阿禾想抢着当领头的。老人没有拦他，只把手放到耳边，做了一个“听”的动作。

阿禾停住，先听小鸟的长音，又听小兔的低鼓。等到中间空出一小格，他才轻轻敲了一下。

### 第12页

那一下不响，却刚好接住前一声。大家没有争谁最大声，曲子反而更好听。

风从山谷里出来，把最后一点回声送到孩子们脚边。

## 回扣

### 第13页

第二章说“美”和“不美”相对而生，也说“音声相和”。西施和东施的故事，让阿禾看见：固定一种美，容易逼人模仿。

山谷里的声音又告诉他：不同的声音不用变成一样，也能彼此应和。

### 第14页

所以“无为”不是不管，而是不把自己的标准硬压上去。花像花，笑像笑，鼓像鼓，笛像笛。

事情成了，也不急着说“都是我的功劳”。一首曲子能留下来，比谁站在最前面更重要。

### 第15页

阿禾在本子上写：不要把一种样子叫作全部的美。不要让一个声音盖住所有声音。

我愿意先听一听，再轻轻加入。这样，世界也许会自己唱起来。

# Chapter 002-2: Xi Shi's Brow and the Echo in the Valley

## Allusion

### Page 1

Zhuangzi tells a story about Xi Shi, a woman people in ancient China called beautiful. One day her chest hurt, so she gently pressed her hand there and frowned.

People passing by still found her moving. It was not a pose she had practiced; it was what naturally appeared when her body was in pain.

### Page 2

Dong Shi, who lived in the same village, saw this and thought, "So pressing the chest and frowning is beauty." She copied the gesture and made sure people saw her.

People did not feel drawn to it. They were startled and moved away. The story became known as "Dong Shi imitates the frown."

### Page 3

This story is not for mocking anyone's looks. It reminds us that if we copy only the outside of another person, without knowing where it comes from, we become less natural, not more beautiful.

That fits the opening of Chapter Two: when everyone accepts only one kind of "beauty," many different forms are pushed into "not beautiful."

## Main Text

### Page 4

After hearing the story, Ahe cheerfully held a "Most Beautiful Frown Contest." Little Rabbit frowned, Little Bird frowned, and Little Stone squeezed his eyebrows together.

Before long, everyone's face ached. The yard was sunny, but it felt as if a small gloomy cloud had drifted in.

### Page 5

Just then, a bean sprout pushed through the soil and opened one tender leaf. Little Rabbit forgot to frown and broke into a grin.

Ahe saw the smile and felt something brighten inside. The most moving expressions are often not the ones we rehearse.

### Page 6

The old man said, "Xi Shi's frown moved people because it was true in that moment. Dong Shi's mistake was pasting someone else's natural gesture onto her own face."

Ahe looked at everyone's tired eyebrows and quickly took down the contest sign.

### Page 7

That afternoon, they walked to the mouth of the valley. Little Stone clapped once, and the sound flew inward.

After a moment, the valley sent it back: clap. The children laughed. Even a sound could go out and return.

### Page 8

Little Bird blew a high note, and Little Rabbit tapped a low drum. The high note did not cover the drum, and the drum did not steal the high note.

One flashed above, one held below. As they listened, it was as if a small bridge were being built in the air.

### Page 9

The ancient *Record of Music* says that when the heart is touched by outside things, it sends out sound. When sounds answer one another and slowly form a pattern, they become music.

Ahe understood a little: one sound ringing alone is not yet music. It has to hear other sounds before it knows where to land.

### Page 10

They tried only one drum: dong, dong, dong. It was loud, but after a while it felt thin.

Then they added bamboo pipe, stone chips, and clapping hands. The sounds began to make room for one another. The little tune was not made by one person; it grew among them.

### Page 11

Ahe wanted to rush in as leader. The old man did not stop him. He only cupped a hand to his ear, the sign for "listen."

Ahe paused. He listened to Little Bird's long note, then to Little Rabbit's low drum. When a small space opened in the middle, he tapped softly once.

### Page 12

The tap was not loud, but it caught the sound before it. No one fought to be the loudest, and the tune became lovelier.

Wind came out of the valley and carried the last echo to the children's feet.

## Return to the Original

### Page 13

Chapter Two says beauty and not-beauty arise in relation, and that notes and tones become harmonious together. Xi Shi and Dong Shi show Ahe how one fixed beauty can push people into imitation.

The valley sounds show him something else: different voices do not have to become the same in order to answer one another.

### Page 14

So "without doing anything" does not mean neglect. It means not pressing your own standard onto everything. Let a flower be a flower, a smile be a smile, a drum be a drum, and a flute be a flute.

When the work is done, do not hurry to say, "That was all mine." A song that remains matters more than the person standing in front.

### Page 15

Ahe wrote in his notebook: Do not call one shape the whole of beauty. Do not let one sound cover every other sound.

I will listen first, then join softly. Perhaps the world will begin to sing by itself.
