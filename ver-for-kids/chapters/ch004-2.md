# 第004-2章：不刺眼的光和看不见的深处

## 典故引入

### 第1页

庄子讲过“空船”的故事。一个人坐船过河，被一只无人船轻轻撞到，多半只是伸手把它撑开。

可如果船上站着一个人，他就容易生气，觉得对方一定是故意撞他。

### 第2页

撞击没有变，变的是心里多出一个尖角：“他在冒犯我。”

第四章说“挫其锐”，不是把人磨成没骨头，而是先看见自己的锋芒从哪里冒出来。

### 第3页

唐朝安史之乱后，郭子仪功劳很大，声望也很高。皇帝听了谗言，担心这位老将军兵权太重，忽然收走他的军队。

有功被疑，手里又有旧部，这正是最容易刺起来的时候。

### 第4页

郭子仪没有召集旧部，也没有拍桌自辩。他奉命去修皇陵，又把旧诏书和机密文书一件件交还。

这些东西若留在手里，可能被人看成刀。他把它们放到阳光下，疑心就少了可以抓住的影子。

### 第5页

后来，仇人挖了郭子仪父亲的坟。朝廷都以为他一定会报复，事情可能牵出一串杀戮。

他却先责备自己：自己带兵多年，士兵也曾扰民伤墓；今天遇到这样的事，是自己的德行还不够。

### 第6页

别人等着看刀光，他先收回了复仇的火。锋芒一挫下去，纷乱就少了一条继续滚大的坡。

这也是“解纷”：有时不是赢过对方，而是让怨气没有地方继续添柴。

### 第7页

李泌面对安史叛军时，也懂这个道理。叛军来势很凶，像一把刚磨亮的刀。

他看见那把刀的弱处：许多人是为抢掠而来，锐是锐，却不能长久忍耐。

### 第8页

他的计策不是正面硬撞，而是断要路、守关键，让敌军在范阳和长安之间来回奔命。

锋芒若总找不到地方刺进去，就会在奔跑、饥疲和拖延里慢慢钝下来。

### 第9页

可皇帝急着收复两京，没有完全听从。眼前的捷报来得快，深处的乱根却没有除净。

第四章说“解其纷”，不是只砍表面的结。线头若还在暗处，过些时候又会缠起来。

### 第10页

再看荷兰画家维米尔。他住在小城代尔夫特，常画的不是英雄和王冠，而是窗边的牛奶、信和安静的脸。

他的光很轻，像怕惊动桌上的灰尘。

### 第11页

《戴珍珠耳环的少女》没有战场，也没有大典。只是一个女孩回头，暗处有一颗小小的珍珠。

可几百年后，人们仍愿意在画前停下。柔光没有逼人看，却把人慢慢请进画里。

### 第12页

这就是“和其光，同其尘”。光没有熄灭，只是不再炫耀自己。

它走进普通房间，照见普通人，也照见尘土。尘土不再只是脏东西，也成了画的一部分。

### 第13页

贝多芬的故事更沉。一个作曲家，后来几乎听不见外面的声音。

对音乐家来说，这像整座世界慢慢关门：掌声远了，乐队远了，连自己的琴声也远了。

### 第14页

他没有把痛苦变成空喊。外面的声音退远后，心里的声音仍在生长。

晚年《第九交响曲》首演时，他背对观众，听不见掌声，却看见所有人站了起来。

### 第15页

那一刻，“湛兮似或存”变得很清楚。真正深的声音，外面好像没有了，里面却还在。

它不靠耳朵证明自己，却能抵达许多人的心。

## 回扣

### 第16页

这些故事并不是散开的。空船让“我被冒犯了”的尖角落下；郭子仪让皇帝的疑心和自己的仇恨少了燃料。

这叫挫锐，不是折断力量，而是让力量不先伤人。

### 第17页

李泌的谋略是在解纷：不和锋芒硬撞，而是看见线头在哪里、燃料在哪里、时间会怎样改变局势。

真正深的解法，常常不是快刀一挥，而是让乱线没有机会越缠越死。

### 第18页

维米尔和贝多芬让阿禾看见“和光同尘”“湛兮似存”。一个把光放进普通房间，一个在失聪后守住更深的声音。

深的东西不急着站上高台，却能长久地照人、动人。

### 第19页

第四章说，道不塞满自己，所以能用；不刺伤世界，所以能入世。

阿禾写下：有空，水才来；不尖，结才松；光柔，尘土也能亮。

# Chapter 004-2: Gentle Light and the Invisible Depth

## Allusion

### Page 1

Zhuangzi tells the story of an empty boat. A person crossing a river is bumped by a boat with no one inside, and he will probably just push it away.

But if someone stands in that boat, anger easily rises. He thinks the person must have hit him on purpose.

### Page 2

The bump has not changed. What changed is that a sharp point has appeared in the heart: "He is offending me."

Chapter Four says, "Blunt sharpness." This does not mean becoming weak. It means first seeing where our own sharpness comes from.

### Page 3

After the An Lushan Rebellion, Guo Ziyi had great merit and a powerful reputation. The emperor heard slander, feared that the old general held too much military power, and suddenly took away his army.

Being doubted after great service, with old troops still loyal to him, was exactly the kind of moment when a person could become sharp.

### Page 4

Guo Ziyi did not summon former soldiers or slam the table in defense. He obeyed an order to repair the imperial tombs, then returned old imperial letters and secret documents one by one.

If those things stayed in his hands, people might see them as blades. By placing them in the sunlight, he left suspicion fewer shadows to grasp.

### Page 5

Later, an enemy dug up Guo Ziyi's father's grave. The court expected Guo to take revenge, and revenge could have pulled many people into killing.

But Guo first blamed himself: in years of war, soldiers under him had also disturbed people and harmed graves. Now this grief showed that his own virtue was not enough.

### Page 6

People waited to see blades; he pulled the fire of revenge back. Once sharpness softened, disorder had less slope on which to roll.

This too is "unraveling complications." Sometimes it is not about defeating the other person, but about giving resentment no more fuel.

### Page 7

Li Bi understood this when he faced the rebel armies. The rebels came fiercely, like a newly sharpened blade.

He saw the weakness inside that blade: many had come for plunder. They were sharp, but they could not endure for long.

### Page 8

His plan was not to crash into them head-on. He would cut key roads and hold key places, making them run between Fanyang and Chang'an.

If sharpness never finds a place to stab, it slowly grows dull through running, hunger, and delay.

### Page 9

But the emperor wanted the two capitals back quickly and did not fully follow Li Bi. Quick victories came, but the deeper root of disorder was not removed.

Chapter Four's "unraveling complications" does not mean cutting only the surface knot. If the hidden thread remains, it will tangle again later.

### Page 10

Now look at the Dutch painter Vermeer. He lived in the small city of Delft and often painted not heroes or crowns, but milk by a window, letters, and quiet faces.

His light is gentle, as if he does not want to startle the dust on the table.

### Page 11

*Girl with a Pearl Earring* has no battlefield and no ceremony. It is only a girl turning her head, with a small pearl in the dark.

Yet centuries later, people still stop before it. The soft light does not force them to look; it slowly invites them inside.

### Page 12

This is "tempering brightness and joining the dust." The light is not put out; it stops showing itself off.

It enters an ordinary room and illuminates ordinary people, and even dust becomes part of the painting.

### Page 13

Beethoven's story is heavier. A composer later became almost unable to hear the outer world.

For a musician, this was like the whole world slowly closing its doors: applause moved away, orchestras moved away, even his own piano moved away.

### Page 14

He did not turn pain into empty shouting. As outer sounds retreated, an inner sound kept growing.

In his later years, at the first performance of the Ninth Symphony, he stood with his back to the audience. He could not hear the applause, but he saw everyone standing.

### Page 15

At that moment, "pure and still, as if present" became clear. The deepest sound seemed gone outside, yet it remained within.

It did not prove itself through ears, but it reached many hearts.

## Return to the Original

### Page 16

These stories are not separate. The empty boat lets the sharp point of "I have been offended" fall away; Guo Ziyi removes fuel from the emperor's suspicion and from his own revenge.

This is blunting sharpness: not breaking strength, but keeping strength from wounding first.

### Page 17

Li Bi's strategy is unraveling complications: do not crash into sharpness; see where the thread is, where the fuel is, and how time changes the situation.

The deepest solution is often not one fast cut. It is keeping tangled threads from becoming a dead knot.

### Page 18

Vermeer and Beethoven show Ahe "tempering brightness," "joining the dust," and "being present in hidden depth." One places light in ordinary rooms; one keeps a deeper sound after losing hearing.

Deep things do not hurry to stand on high platforms, yet they can shine and move people for a long time.

### Page 19

Chapter Four says Dao does not stuff itself full, so it can be used; it does not wound the world, so it can enter the world.

Ahe wrote: Where there is room, water comes. Where sharpness softens, knots loosen. Where light is gentle, even dust can shine.
